Cash Payment vs. 1099 Compliance Check

Captured: April 12, 2026  |  Priority: Review Before Next Payment

⚠ Requires review before making any cash payments — IRS rules apply

The Question

Can Norris Utilities® pay a worker in cash without issuing a 1099-NEC? This applies to any independent contractor or service provider currently being considered for a cash arrangement.

IRS Rules on Cash Payments

  • Cash payments are legal — but the IRS still requires reporting if total payments to a single person reach $600 or more in a calendar year (Form 1099-NEC).
  • Paying cash does not eliminate the 1099 requirement. The method of payment (cash, check, Venmo, Zelle) does not matter — only the total dollar amount matters.
  • If the worker is paid under $600 total for the entire calendar year, no 1099-NEC is required. The worker still owes taxes on the income, but Norris Utilities® has no filing obligation.
  • If the worker is a corporation (S-Corp or C-Corp), a 1099-NEC is generally not required regardless of amount (with limited exceptions for legal/medical services).

Risk of Paying Cash Without a 1099

If total payments hit $600+ and no 1099-NEC is filed, the IRS can impose penalties of $60–$310 per form (2026 rates), with higher penalties for intentional disregard. Alabama follows the same threshold. Additionally, if the worker later claims unemployment or disability and references Norris Utilities® as an employer, it could trigger a misclassification audit.

When Cash Payment Without 1099 Is Fine

  • Total payments to the individual will stay under $600 for all of 2026
  • The worker is a corporation (get a W-9 to confirm entity type)
  • The payment is for a personal expense, not a Norris Utilities® business expense

Recommended Next Steps

1
Get a W-9 from the worker. This confirms their legal name, SSN or EIN, and entity type. Keep it on file. This protects Norris Utilities® regardless of payment method.
2
Estimate total payments for 2026. If you expect to pay her $600 or more this year, plan on filing a 1099-NEC by January 31, 2027. Cash or not, the form is required.
3
Track every payment. Record the date, amount, and purpose of each cash payment. A simple note in the books is sufficient. Caroline can maintain a running log.
4
Confirm with Patrick Lavette or your CPA. For amounts approaching $600, a 2-minute call to confirm the filing plan is worth the peace of mind.
