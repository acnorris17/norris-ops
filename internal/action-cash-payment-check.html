Cash Payment — Check Feasibility vs. 1099

Captured April 13, 2026 — Norris Utilities®

Task Summary

Determine whether it is possible and advisable to pay her in cash rather than issuing a 1099 for contractor compensation. This requires understanding IRS reporting thresholds, state labor law requirements, and the practical implications for Norris Utilities® bookkeeping.

Action Steps

  • Confirm total expected payments for the calendar year 2026 — if $600 or more, a 1099-NEC is required by the IRS regardless of payment method (cash, check, or direct deposit)
  • Verify that paying in cash does not change the 1099 reporting obligation — the IRS requires reporting based on total amount paid, not the method of payment
  • Consult with your CPA or accountant to confirm Alabama-specific requirements for cash payments to contractors
  • If total payments will be under $600 for the year, cash payment without a 1099 is permissible — but still should be documented internally
  • Regardless of payment method, keep written records: date, amount, description of work performed, and recipient name
  • Get a signed receipt for every cash payment made

Key Considerations

IRS Rules at a Glance

  • $600 threshold: If you pay any non-employee $600 or more in a tax year, you must file a 1099-NEC — this applies whether you pay by cash, check, or bank transfer
  • Cash is not a loophole: The payment method does not exempt you from reporting requirements
  • Record keeping: The IRS expects businesses to maintain records of all payments to contractors regardless of amount
  • Worker classification: Ensure she is properly classified as an independent contractor, not an employee — misclassification carries significant penalties

Important

Paying in cash to avoid issuing a 1099 when one is legally required is a violation of IRS reporting rules and can result in penalties. If total payments will reach $600 or more this year, a 1099-NEC must be filed regardless of how payment is made. Confirm the expected total and consult your accountant before proceeding.

Recommended Next Step

Call your CPA or accountant to confirm the total expected payments for this person in 2026. If the total will be under $600, cash payment is fine — just keep receipts. If $600 or more, a 1099-NEC is required no matter the payment method, and you should continue the current process.
