Negotiation Approach

Phase 1 — Research
Pull comparable sales, tax records, and inspection history for the property and surrounding area
Phase 2 — Initial Offer
Submit initial offer based on comps analysis, condition assessment, and market positioning
Phase 3 — Due Diligence
Full inspection, title search, survey, and appraisal contingencies
Phase 4 — Close
Finalize terms, coordinate with Patrick Lavette at Renasant Bank for financing, and close

Action Items

1

Pull Comparable Sales

Research recent sales of comparable properties in the Buttewoods area. Focus on homes 5,000–8,000 sq ft sold within the last 12 months to establish fair market value range.

2

Review Tax & Title Records

Obtain county tax assessment records, verify clear title, check for liens or easements, and confirm legal property boundaries and lot size.

3

Schedule Property Inspection

Arrange a full home inspection covering structural, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and roof systems. At 7,098 sq ft, attention to deferred maintenance is critical.

4

Contact Renasant Bank

Reach out to Patrick Lavette at Renasant Bank to discuss pre-approval, financing options, and rate lock timing for the acquisition.

5

Prepare Initial Offer

Draft purchase offer with appropriate contingencies — inspection, financing, and appraisal. Include a competitive earnest money deposit to demonstrate seriousness.

6

Negotiate Terms

Be prepared for counteroffers. Key leverage points: cash position, flexible closing timeline, and willingness to accept the property in current condition if priced right.

Due Diligence Checklist

  • Comparable sales analysis (5,000–8,000 sq ft, 12-month window)
  • County tax assessment and property records
  • Title search — verify clear title
  • Survey / plat verification
  • HOA covenants and restrictions review
  • Zoning verification
  • Full home inspection (structural, mechanical, systems)
  • Roof age and condition report
  • Termite / pest inspection
  • Appraisal ordered through lender
  • Insurance quotes obtained
  • Financing pre-approval confirmed with Renasant

Negotiation Notes

At 7,098 sq ft, this is a significant estate property. Key strategy: lead with a strong but fair offer backed by comps data. Emphasize buyer reliability and flexible timeline. Coordinate all financing through Patrick Lavette at Renasant Bank. Keep earnest money competitive to show commitment. If inspection reveals major issues, use documented findings as leverage for price adjustment rather than walking away — the square footage and location make this worth pursuing strategically.
