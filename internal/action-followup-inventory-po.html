Action Required

Follow Up on Last PO Order for Inventory

Captured April 12, 2026 — Norris Utilities® reMarkable Action Item

What Needs to Happen

Contact the supplier to get a status update on the most recent purchase order placed for inventory replenishment. Confirm the order is being processed, verify expected ship dates, and ensure quantities match what was originally ordered. If there are any delays, backorders, or substitutions, document them and determine the impact on current stock levels and pending customer commitments.

Why This Matters

Inventory availability is the foundation of Norris Utilities® customer service. When a customer needs FlexPro Armor bucket covers, Samson Rope, or any other product, the ability to ship same-day or next-day from stock on hand is a competitive advantage. A delayed or lost PO means empty shelves, missed delivery promises, and lost trust with customers who depend on Norris Utilities® to deliver.

Following up promptly on POs also catches errors early — wrong quantities, incorrect SKUs, or pricing discrepancies are far easier to resolve before product ships than after.

Follow-Up Steps

  1. 1 Locate the most recent PO number, date, and supplier from records (email, QuickBooks, or order confirmations)
  2. 2 Contact the supplier directly by phone or email to request a status update on the order
  3. 3 Confirm all line items: SKUs, quantities, and unit pricing match the original PO
  4. 4 Get the expected ship date and tracking information if already shipped
  5. 5 Ask about any backorders, delays, or substitutions — document everything
  6. 6 Update internal inventory tracker with confirmed delivery timeline
  7. 7 If any items are delayed, assess impact on pending customer orders and communicate proactively

Key Details to Verify

  • PO number confirmed and on file with the supplier
  • All ordered quantities match the original PO (no partial fills without approval)
  • Pricing matches the agreed-upon rates — no unexpected surcharges or price increases
  • Ship date confirmed and within the expected delivery window
  • Shipping method and carrier confirmed (verify freight vs. ground if applicable)
  • Tracking number obtained (or estimated availability date for tracking)
  • No items on backorder or discontinued — if so, get revised ETA or alternative
