Action Required

Follow Up on Last PO for Inventory Order

Captured April 11, 2026 — Norris Utilities® reMarkable Action Item

What Needs to Happen

Follow up on the most recent purchase order submitted for inventory replenishment. Confirm the PO was received by the supplier, verify expected ship dates, and ensure the order is on track to arrive as planned. If there are any delays, backorders, or discrepancies, identify them now before they impact customer commitments.

Why This Matters

Maintaining adequate inventory is critical for Norris Utilities® to fulfill customer orders without delay. As the sole sales force, Aaron needs to know exactly what stock is incoming and when it will arrive so he can confidently commit delivery timelines to customers. A missed or delayed PO can mean lost sales and damaged relationships with utilities that depend on timely equipment delivery.

Steps to Complete

  1. 1 Pull the most recent purchase order number and details from records — confirm supplier, line items, quantities, and dollar amounts
  2. 2 Contact the supplier to confirm the PO was received and is being processed
  3. 3 Request current estimated ship date and tracking information if available
  4. 4 Ask about any backorders, partial shipments, or items that may be delayed beyond the original timeline
  5. 5 Confirm payment terms and whether any invoices are outstanding that could hold the order
  6. 6 Report back to Aaron with a summary: what is confirmed, what is shipping, and any issues that need attention

Key Details to Verify

When speaking with the supplier, confirm the following for every line item on the PO:

PO Number & Date Submitted
Confirm the supplier has the correct PO on file
Ship Date & Tracking
Get estimated or confirmed delivery date and carrier info
Quantities & Line Items
Verify all items and quantities match what was ordered
Status: Pending — Awaiting follow-up with supplier on last PO
