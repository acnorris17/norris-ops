Action Item

Verify Peace of Mind Maintenance Program Requirement

Captured: April 13, 2026 | Source: reMarkable Notes
⚠
Medium Priority — Needs Clarification
Determine whether the Peace of Mind maintenance program is a contractual requirement or optional add-on

What Needs to Happen

Confirm whether the Peace of Mind maintenance program is a mandatory requirement for any current or pending equipment purchase, warranty compliance, or service agreement. This includes verifying whether the program is required by the OEM, required by the customer's internal policy, or simply a recommended option.

Steps to Complete

  • Review the original equipment purchase agreement or quote to check for maintenance program stipulations
  • Contact the OEM or dealer representative to confirm whether Peace of Mind enrollment is a warranty requirement
  • Determine if the customer has an internal policy requiring extended maintenance coverage on purchased equipment
  • Compare the cost of the Peace of Mind program against self-managing maintenance to evaluate the value proposition
  • Document the findings and make a recommendation on whether to enroll or decline

Key Questions to Answer

  • Is the Peace of Mind program required to maintain the manufacturer warranty?
  • Does the purchase contract or financing agreement mandate enrollment?
  • What does the program cover versus standard warranty?
  • What is the total cost of the program over the equipment lifecycle?
  • Are there penalties or coverage gaps if the program is not purchased?

Expected Outcome

A clear, documented answer on whether the Peace of Mind maintenance program is required or optional. If required, identify by whom and under what terms. If optional, provide a cost-benefit summary so Aaron can make an informed decision on enrollment.
