Action Details

Get the exact definition of what "lifetime warranty" means from H2O Waterproofing. "Lifetime" can mean different things depending on the contractor — it could be the lifetime of the structure, the lifetime of the original owner, or a fixed number of years that they call "lifetime."

You need in writing: what specific coverage the lifetime warranty provides, what is excluded, whether it transfers to a new owner if the property is sold, and what conditions could void the warranty. Do not close until this is documented.

Next Steps

  1. Call H2O Waterproofing Ask specifically: "What does your lifetime warranty cover, and what does 'lifetime' mean — whose lifetime, and how many years?"
  2. Get the Definition in Writing Request a written document or email stating the exact definition of their lifetime warranty — duration, coverage scope, and what "lifetime" refers to (structure, owner, or fixed term).
  3. Ask About Exclusions What voids the warranty? What is NOT covered? Are there maintenance requirements you must follow to keep coverage active?
  4. Ask About Transferability If the property is sold, does the lifetime warranty transfer to the new owner, or does it end with the current owner?
  5. Ask About Claims Process How do you file a warranty claim? What is the response time? Do they cover labor and materials, or just materials?
  6. File the Written Response Save the warranty definition document with your property/vendor records. Do not proceed to closing without this on file.
Important: Do not accept a verbal explanation of the lifetime warranty. If H2O Waterproofing does not have a formal warranty document, ask them to put the definition, coverage, and exclusions in a written letter or email. Do not close until the lifetime warranty coverage is fully defined and documented in writing.
