Addendum Review Checklist
  • Read the full addendum language covering basement water intrusion disclosure, remediation responsibilities, and liability allocation
  • Verify the addendum clearly defines what constitutes "water intrusion" (seepage, standing water, moisture, mold presence)
  • Confirm seller disclosure obligations — has the seller disclosed any prior water intrusion events, repairs, or insurance claims
  • Review remediation cost responsibility — who bears the cost if water intrusion is discovered before or after closing
  • Check inspection contingency terms — confirm the right to a professional waterproofing or structural inspection
  • Identify any warranties or guarantees included for prior waterproofing work (transferability, duration, scope)
  • Review hold-harmless or indemnification clauses — ensure they do not waive buyer protections
  • Confirm addendum aligns with Alabama real estate disclosure law and does not conflict with the primary purchase agreement
Key Terms to Verify
Disclosure Scope
Past water events, repairs, and insurance claims related to basement
Remediation Responsibility
Confirm whether seller or buyer is responsible for future issues
Inspection Rights
Right to waterproofing inspection and timeline for completion
Warranty Transfer
Any existing waterproofing warranties and transferability to buyer
Liability Limits
Caps on remediation costs or liability after closing
Contingency Deadline
Date by which inspection must be completed and objections raised
