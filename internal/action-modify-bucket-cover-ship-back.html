Sizing Discrepancy

What We Shipped vs. What Fits

Confirm the measured bucket interior against the cover we delivered. The competitor-to-NU size translation (24×48 → 28×51) is the usual pinch point — call the site lead and measure twice before re-cutting.

SKU / Reference Nominal Size Actual Inside Dim Status
NU-BC-2851 (2-Man) 28" × 51" Confirm with customer Shipped unit

Customer Requirement To Be Verified Measure tape photo Source of truth
Before cutting: request a tape-measure photo from the customer covering length, width, and top-rail depth. Never modify off a verbal dimension.

Modification Plan

Shop Workflow

  • Recall the unit from the customer with a prepaid inbound label.
  • Inspect for field wear — any abrasion or contamination gets logged in the quality file.
  • Re-cut shell panels to the verified dimensions; preserve the reinforced hem line.
  • Re-stitch using the matched bonded thread (no substitutions — warranty condition).
  • Re-attach grommets, label, and phoenix badge in original positions.

Quality Gate

  • Weigh finished cover and compare to SKU target weight.
  • Lay cover over a calibrated jig at the new dimensions — confirm drop and bead seat.
  • Photograph all four sides for the customer file and the production binder.
  • Serial-tag as a "FIELD MOD" in the remake log so warranty history stays traceable.

Return Shipping

Ship-Back Logistics

  • Ship UPS Ground or FedEx Ground — whichever matches the customer's receiving dock hours.
  • Apply Ben's shipping formula for the return freight charge: CustomerCost = ROUNDUP( ROUNDUP(RegCost, 0) × 1.10, 0 ).
  • Reuse the original bag and box only if undamaged; otherwise new packaging, new desiccant.
  • Include a printed care/warranty insert and a handwritten note from Aaron.
  • Email tracking to the customer the moment the label scans — not at end of day.
Tone: we own this. No blame language in the cover note. "Here is the corrected unit — appreciate your patience while we made it right."
