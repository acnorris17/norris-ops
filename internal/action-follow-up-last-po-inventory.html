Receive & Process Inventory from Last PO

Inventory arriving — receive shipment, verify contents against PO, update stock records, and store properly.
Action Type
Inventory Receiving
Priority
High — Time-Sensitive
Date Created
April 13, 2026
Status
Pending — Awaiting Receipt

Action Details

Inventory from the most recent purchase order is expected to arrive. When the shipment comes in, it must be received properly: inspect the packaging for damage, verify every item and quantity against the original PO, and check for any backorders or shortages.

Once verified, update the internal inventory records with the new stock counts. Any discrepancies between what was ordered and what was received must be documented and reported to the supplier immediately. Do not put product into storage until it has been fully checked in.

Receiving Process

  1. Inspect Shipment on Arrival Check all boxes and packaging for visible damage before signing the delivery receipt. Photograph any damage immediately. Note the carrier name, tracking number, and number of boxes received.
  2. Pull the Original PO Retrieve the purchase order that corresponds to this shipment. Have the PO number, line items, quantities, and expected pricing ready for cross-reference.
  3. Verify Contents Against PO Open each box and count every item. Compare part numbers, descriptions, and quantities to the PO line by line. Mark each line item as received, short, or over-shipped.
  4. Document Any Discrepancies If quantities do not match, items are damaged, or wrong product was shipped, document it with photos and notes. Contact the supplier the same day to file a claim or request a corrective shipment.
  5. Update Inventory Records Enter the received quantities into the inventory tracking system. Update stock counts, note lot numbers or serial numbers if applicable, and record the date received.
  6. Store Product Properly Move verified inventory to the correct storage location. Ensure items are organized, labeled, and accessible for fulfillment. FlexPro Armor covers should be stored flat or hanging to prevent creasing.

Receiving Checklist

  • Delivery receipt signed with noted exceptions
  • All boxes counted and inspected for external damage
  • Contents verified against PO line by line
  • Discrepancies documented and reported to supplier
  • Inventory records updated with received quantities
  • Product stored in correct location
  • Packing slip filed with PO documentation
Reminder: Do not assume the shipment is complete until every line item on the PO has been physically verified. If the supplier sent a partial shipment, confirm the remaining items have a ship date. Keep the PO open until every item has been received and accounted for.
