    <!-- 3. LEVERAGE -->
    <section class="nu-section">
      <h2 class="nu-section-title">
        <span class="nu-section-number">3</span>
        <span><span class="nu-section-title-first">Leverage</span> <span class="nu-section-title-rest">Map — Ours vs. Theirs</span></span>
      </h2>
      <div class="nu-leverage-grid">
        <div class="nu-leverage-block ours">
          <div class="nu-leverage-header">Norris Leverage (Use These)</div>
          <ul class="nu-leverage-list">
            <li>Strong financials, third-generation operating business — pre-approval letter or proof of funds in hand at offer.</li>
            <li>Flexible close timeline — can accommodate seller's preferred move-out date.</li>
            <li>Clean offer — minimal contingencies beyond inspection, financing, and appraisal.</li>
            <li>No home-sale contingency. Not waiting on another property to sell.</li>
            <li>Local Birmingham buyer with documented business presence — sellers prefer in-market closers.</li>
            <li>Willingness to deposit serious earnest money to demonstrate intent.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-leverage-block theirs">
          <div class="nu-leverage-header">Seller Leverage (Watch For)</div>
          <ul class="nu-leverage-list">
            <li>Days on market — short DOM strengthens seller; long DOM strengthens buyer.</li>
            <li>Multiple offer scenario — confirm in writing before raising offer; ask for highest-and-best date.</li>
            <li>Recent comparable sales above list price.</li>
            <li>Personal attachment — heirs, original owner, or seller emotionally invested in price.</li>
            <li>Existing under-market mortgage rate that could be assumable (rare, verify if possible).</li>
            <li>Off-market or pocket interest — pressure tactic, often unverifiable.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>
    </section>

      <h2 class="nu-section-title">
        <span class="nu-section-number">4</span>
        <span><span class="nu-section-title-first">Offer</span> <span class="nu-section-title-rest">Terms Aaron Will Sign</span></span>
      </h2>
      <div class="nu-table-wrap">
        <table class="nu-table">
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Earnest Money</td>
              <td>1.0% – 1.5% of purchase price held in escrow. Refundable through inspection and financing periods.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Inspection Period</td>
              <td>10 business days. Right to renegotiate or void for material defects.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Financing Contingency</td>
              <td>21 days. Conventional financing with 25–30% down. Lender pre-underwritten.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Appraisal Contingency</td>
              <td>Required. Appraisal must support purchase price; renegotiate if low.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Closing Date</td>
              <td>30–45 days from acceptance. Flexible — willing to lengthen to accommodate seller move-out.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Seller Concessions</td>
              <td>Request seller contribution toward closing costs OR repairs identified in inspection — not both.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Possession</td>
              <td>At funding and recording. Post-closing occupancy by seller only with written rent-back at fair market rate.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Inclusions</td>
              <td>Built-ins, appliances, light fixtures, window treatments. Itemize anything custom or expensive in the offer.</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td class="label-cell">Title &amp; Survey</td>
              <td>Buyer's choice of closing attorney and title insurance. New survey at buyer's expense if needed.</td>
            </tr>

    <section class="nu-section">
      <h2 class="nu-section-title">
        <span class="nu-section-number">5</span>
        <span><span class="nu-section-title-first">Talking</span> <span class="nu-section-title-rest">Points &amp; Negotiation Script</span></span>
      </h2>

      <div class="nu-script-card">
        <div class="nu-script-label">Opening — When Submitting the Offer</div>
        <div class="nu-script-text">We've done our homework on Buttewoods. This is a serious, clean offer from a Birmingham buyer who can close on the seller's timeline. The number reflects the comps and our inspection expectations on a 7,098 square foot home. We're prepared to move quickly if we're aligned on price.</div>
      </div>

      <div class="nu-script-card">
        <div class="nu-script-label">Counter — When They Push Back on Price</div>
        <div class="nu-script-text">I appreciate the response. Help me understand what comps support that number — I'm working off three closed sales in the last six months that put market value below list. I've got real flexibility on terms and timeline, but the price has to track the data.</div>
      </div>

      <div class="nu-script-card">
        <div class="nu-script-label">Inspection Renegotiation — After Findings</div>
        <div class="nu-script-text">The inspection report flagged items totaling roughly [dollar amount]. I'm not asking the seller to fix everything — I'd rather take a credit at closing in that amount and handle the work my way. That keeps the deal moving and gets us to close on schedule.</div>
      </div>

      <div class="nu-script-card">
        <div class="nu-script-label">Walk-Away — When the Number Stalls</div>
        <div class="nu-script-text">I respect where you are. I'm not the buyer who keeps inching up. My number is my number, and the offer stays open for 48 hours. If the seller decides to revisit, I'm here. Otherwise, we wish them well.</div>
      </div>
    </section>

    <!-- 6. TIMELINE -->
    <section class="nu-section">
      <h2 class="nu-section-title">
        <span class="nu-section-number">6</span>
        <span><span class="nu-section-title-first">Negotiation</span> <span class="nu-section-title-rest">Timeline &amp; Milestones</span></span>
      </h2>
      <div class="nu-timeline">
        <div class="nu-timeline-item done">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">1</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 0 — Pre-Offer</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Comp Pull, Walk-Through, Pre-Approval</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Buyer's agent assembles closed comps within 3 miles. Lender issues current pre-approval letter. Aaron sets opening, target, and walk-away in writing.</div>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-timeline-item">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">2</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 1 — Offer Submission</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Submit Written Offer with Pre-Approval</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Offer presented with pre-approval letter, earnest money commitment, and 48-hour response window. Buyer's agent calls listing agent to walk through strengths.</div>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-timeline-item">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">3</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 2–4 — Counter Round</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Counter, Hold Position, Counter Again if Needed</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Expect one to two counters. Each move comes off opening toward target — never above target without seller concession of equal value (closing credit, inclusions, repairs).</div>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-timeline-item">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">4</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 5 — Mutual Acceptance</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Contract Signed, Earnest Deposited</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Earnest money wired to escrow. Inspection scheduled. Lender file moves to underwriting.</div>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-timeline-item">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">5</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 6–15 — Inspection</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Inspection Period &amp; Repair Negotiation</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Full inspection plus specialty trades. Negotiate credit-at-closing or seller-completed repairs for material findings. Decide go/no-go before window expires.</div>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-timeline-item">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">6</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 16–28 — Financing &amp; Appraisal</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Appraisal Ordered, Loan Conditions Cleared</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Appraisal must support price. If low, renegotiate or invoke appraisal contingency. Underwriting clears to close.</div>
        </div>
        <div class="nu-timeline-item">
          <div class="nu-timeline-marker">7</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-date">Day 30–45 — Closing</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-title">Final Walk-Through, Funding, Recording</div>
          <div class="nu-timeline-body">Final walk-through 24–48 hours before closing. Sign at closing attorney's office. Lender funds, deed records, possession transfers.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </section>
