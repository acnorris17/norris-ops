Shipping Workflow — Step by Step

Every order follows this exact sequence. No steps are skipped. If something stalls at any step, message Aaron on Telegram.

  1. 1

    Order Comes In

    Aaron receives the order via email, phone call, or through a distributor/dealer channel. Aaron confirms the order details with the customer — product, quantity, ship-to address, and any special instructions.

  2. 2

    Aaron Builds the Shipping Document (SD)

    Aaron creates the SD using the master template. The SD includes: customer name, ship-to address, product SKUs, quantities, order date, and any notes. Aaron saves the completed SD to Google Drive and notifies CB it is ready.

  3. 3

    Aaron Ships the Order

    Aaron coordinates with the supplier or ships directly from inventory. Once shipped, Aaron obtains the tracking number and carrier information (UPS, FedEx, or freight LTL) and sends it to CB.

  4. 4

    CB Enters Tracking in Google Sheets CB Action

    CB opens the shared Google Sheets order tracker. Locate the row for this order (match by customer name and order date). Enter: tracking number, carrier, ship date. Mark the status column as "Shipped".

  5. 5

    CB Creates the QuickBooks Invoice CB Action

    Using the SD as the source of truth, CB creates the invoice in QuickBooks Online. Match the line items exactly to the SD — same SKUs, descriptions, quantities, and prices. Apply the correct customer payment terms. See Section 3 for detailed QB instructions.

  6. 6

    CB Sends the Invoice to the Customer CB Action

    Send the invoice directly from QuickBooks using the customer's email on file. Include the tracking number in the invoice email message body so the customer has everything in one place. BCC Aaron on the invoice email.

  7. 7

    CB Updates Google Sheets Status CB Action

    Return to the Google Sheets tracker. Update the status to "Invoiced". Enter the QB invoice number and the date sent. This closes the loop on that order.

Shipping Cost Formula (Ben's Formula): Customer shipping cost = ROUNDUP(ROUNDUP(RegCost, 0) × 1.10, 0). This rounds the base shipping cost up to the nearest whole dollar, then adds 10%, and rounds up again. Aaron will provide the RegCost figure on the SD.

QuickBooks Invoice Process

Creating the Invoice

  1. Log in to QuickBooks Online at qbo.intuit.com
  2. Click + New → Invoice
  3. Select the customer from the dropdown (if new customer, see below)
  4. Set the Invoice Date to the ship date from the SD
  5. Set Due Date per the customer's payment terms (Net 30 is standard unless Aaron specifies otherwise)
  6. Add each line item from the SD:
    • Product/Service: Match the SKU (e.g., NU-BC-2851)
    • Description: Use the description exactly as written on the SD
    • Quantity: Match the SD
    • Rate: Match the SD price (this already reflects the correct margin)
  7. Add shipping as a separate line item if applicable, using Ben's Formula result
  8. Review the total against the SD to confirm they match
  9. Add a Message on Invoice: "Thank you for your business. Tracking: [NUMBER] via [CARRIER]."
  10. Click Save and Send

New Customer Setup in QuickBooks

If the customer does not exist in QB yet:

  • Click + New → Customer
  • Enter the company name, billing address, shipping address, primary contact name, email, and phone
  • Set payment terms to Net 30 unless Aaron specifies otherwise
  • Save, then return to create the invoice
⚠ Never Do This in QuickBooks
  • Never change a product price without Aaron's direct instruction
  • Never delete an invoice — if an error is made, void it and recreate
  • Never set up new payment terms without Aaron's approval
  • Never apply credits or discounts without Aaron's explicit written approval

What CB Never Does Without Aaron's Approval

These items require Aaron's explicit written approval (Telegram message or email) before any action is taken. No exceptions.

🛑 Requires Aaron's Approval — Always
  • Pricing changes — Never adjust any price on a quote, invoice, or portal document. All pricing flows from Aaron.
  • Customer-facing communications — No emails, calls, or messages to customers or suppliers without Aaron reviewing the content first. Routine invoice sends from QB (using the standard template) are the only exception.
  • Discounts, credits, or refunds — Never apply any discount, credit memo, or refund in QuickBooks without written approval from Aaron.
  • New customer setup with non-standard terms — Net 30 is the default. Any other payment terms require Aaron's approval.
  • Vendor/supplier communications — Do not contact Boss Products, Samson Rope, Skylift, Bay Shore Systems, or any supplier directly unless Aaron specifically instructs it.
  • Creating new Google Drive top-level folders — Use the existing structure. If a new folder is needed, ask Aaron first.
  • Editing or modifying portal files — The Norris Ops portal is managed by Aaron. Report issues; do not attempt to fix them.
  • Social media posts or brand communications — All public-facing content goes through Aaron and MK Smith.
  • Committing to delivery dates — Aaron manages all delivery timelines. Never promise a customer a ship date.
  • Sharing internal pricing, cost data, or margin information — This data is strictly confidential. Never share with customers, suppliers, or anyone outside the company.

When in doubt, ask. It is always better to pause and message Aaron on Telegram than to take an action that needs to be reversed. Aaron checks Telegram constantly starting at 4 AM.
