Get Patent Drawings

Captured April 12, 2026
Priority: Obtain patent drawings to protect Norris Utilities® intellectual property

Overview

Secure professional patent drawings for filing with the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO). Patent drawings are a required component of utility and design patent applications and must meet strict USPTO formatting and technical standards.

Steps to Complete

  • Identify which product(s) or invention(s) require patent drawings
  • Gather all reference materials — photos, CAD files, sketches, and specifications
  • Contact a USPTO-qualified patent illustrator or drafting service
  • Request a quote and timeline for formal patent drawings
  • Review draft drawings for accuracy against the actual product
  • Confirm drawings meet USPTO requirements (black ink on white, proper margins, figure numbering)
  • Deliver final drawings to patent attorney for inclusion in the application

Key Considerations

Patent drawings must show every feature of the invention as described in the claims. Multiple views (front, side, top, perspective, exploded) are typically required. All drawings must be in black and white line art unless color is petitioned for and approved.

Coordinate with your patent attorney to ensure drawings align with the written description and claims before submission.
