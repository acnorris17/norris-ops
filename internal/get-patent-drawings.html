Overview

Obtain official patent drawings for FlexPro Armor bucket cover design. Patent drawings are required to document and protect the proprietary design elements of the FlexPro Armor product line, including the unique construction, fit system, and material configuration that distinguish it from competitors.

Action Steps
  • Identify and contact a registered patent illustrator or patent attorney who can produce USPTO-compliant drawings
  • Gather all FlexPro Armor reference materials — product photos, dimensions (28"×51" and 28"×34"), construction details, and material specs
  • Provide illustrator with physical samples or detailed photographs showing all angles, seams, attachment points, and unique design features
  • Review draft patent drawings for accuracy — confirm all critical design elements are represented
  • Finalize drawings in USPTO-accepted format (black and white line drawings with proper numbering and reference labels)
  • File drawings with patent application or store securely for future filing
Context

Product

FlexPro Armor bucket covers — handmade in the USA. Available in 2-Man (28"×51") and 1.5-Man (28"×34") configurations, plus combo variants.

Why It Matters

Patent drawings establish formal intellectual property protection for the FlexPro Armor design, preventing competitors from copying the proprietary construction and fit system.

SKUs Covered

NU-BC-2851 (2-Man) · NU-BC-2834 (1.5-Man) · NU-BC-2851-C (2-Man Combo) · NU-BC-2834-C (1.5-Man Combo)

Priority

High — IP protection is essential as FlexPro Armor gains market traction. Drawings should be completed before any additional public product disclosures.

Notes

Patent drawings must meet USPTO requirements: black ink on white paper, specific margin sizes, and proper figure numbering. A qualified patent illustrator will ensure compliance. Consider whether to pursue a design patent (protects appearance) or utility patent (protects function), as drawing requirements differ for each type.
