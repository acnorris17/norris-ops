Project Context

Carbon 3D is a digital manufacturing platform that uses continuous liquid interface production (CLIP) technology to produce industrial-grade parts from resin. Norris Utilities® is exploring Carbon 3D as a way to produce custom utility components — mounting brackets, hardware accessories, and potentially FlexPro Armor attachment hardware — faster and more cost-effectively than traditional manufacturing.

Current Progress

Phase 1: Research & Scoping 15%
  • Project scope and deliverables have been identified at a high level
  • Initial research into Carbon 3D capabilities is underway
  • Target parts and products still need to be finalized
  • Vendor pricing and lead time estimates have not yet been requested

Focus Areas for This Session

Target Parts

Identify 3–5 specific utility components that would benefit from Carbon 3D production. Focus on parts currently hard to source, slow to manufacture, or expensive in small quantities.

Material Selection

Review Carbon 3D resin materials for UV resistance, impact strength, and temperature tolerance. Utility field conditions require parts that perform in heat, cold, rain, and sun exposure.

Vendor Outreach

Contact Carbon 3D production partners to request quotes on sample parts. Get pricing per unit at quantities of 50, 250, and 1,000 to evaluate margin potential.

Cost Comparison

Build a side-by-side cost analysis: Carbon 3D vs. injection molding vs. CNC machining. Include tooling costs, per-unit cost, lead time, and minimum order quantities.

Key Questions to Answer

  • Which Norris Utilities® products or accessories have the highest margin opportunity from Carbon 3D production?
  • What is the per-unit cost difference between Carbon 3D and current sourcing methods at realistic order volumes?
  • Can Carbon 3D parts meet the durability requirements for utility field work (UV, impact, temperature extremes)?
  • What is the realistic lead time from design file to finished part delivery?
  • Are there Carbon 3D production partners with utility or industrial experience in the Southeast?

Next Steps

1
Finalize the target parts list. Pick 3–5 components from current product lines or field needs. Prioritize parts with sourcing pain points or high margin potential.
2
Request Carbon 3D material data sheets. Pull technical specs for the top candidate resins — EPU 44 (flexible), RPU 130 (rigid), CE 221 (high-temp) — and compare against field requirements.
3
Get vendor quotes. Reach out to at least two Carbon 3D production partners. Send CAD files or part sketches and request per-unit pricing at 50, 250, and 1,000 units.
4
Build the cost comparison spreadsheet. Carbon 3D vs. traditional manufacturing for each target part. Include tooling amortization, shipping, and margin at Norris Utilities® standard pricing.
5
Order prototype samples. Once pricing is confirmed, order 2–3 samples of the highest-priority part for hands-on evaluation and field testing.
