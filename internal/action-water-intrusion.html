Water Intrusion Issue — Professional Waterproofing Required

Created: April 13, 2026  |  Owner: Aaron C. Norris

Address the water intrusion issue at the property by engaging a professional waterproofing contractor. The scope includes identifying the source of intrusion, obtaining repair estimates, scheduling remediation, and verifying the fix to prevent future damage.
Priority Level: High Water intrusion worsens over time. Delayed response increases cost of repair and risk of structural damage, mold, and equipment loss.

Action Steps

  1. 1
    Document the Intrusion

    Photograph and video all visible water entry points, staining, and moisture damage. Note dates when intrusion was first observed and conditions that trigger it (heavy rain, sustained rain, direction of wind).

  2. 2
    Contact Professional Waterproofing Contractors

    Reach out to at least two licensed waterproofing specialists in the Birmingham area for on-site inspections and written estimates. Request detailed scope of work, timeline, and warranty information.

  3. 3
    Schedule On-Site Inspections

    Coordinate inspections within the next 5 business days. Ensure the contractor evaluates the foundation, exterior walls, roof penetrations, and any drainage or grading issues contributing to the problem.

  4. 4
    Review Estimates and Select Contractor

    Compare estimates on scope, materials, labor, warranty length, and total cost. Verify contractor licensing, insurance, and references before making a selection.

  5. 5
    Schedule and Complete Remediation

    Book the repair work as soon as possible. Confirm start date, expected duration, and any required access or preparation on your end before work begins.

  6. 6
    Verify and Follow Up

    After repair, monitor during the next rain event to confirm the intrusion is resolved. Obtain final documentation, warranty paperwork, and paid invoices for records.

Contractor Selection Criteria

Requirement

Licensed & Insured

Contractor must hold a valid Alabama Home Builder or General Contractor license and carry liability insurance with a minimum $1M coverage.

Requirement

Written Warranty

Require a written warranty of at least 5 years on all waterproofing work, covering both materials and labor.

Preference

Local References

Preference for contractors with verifiable references from commercial or residential projects in the Birmingham metro area.

Completion Checklist

  • Water intrusion documented with photos and notes
  • At least two contractor inspections completed
  • Written estimates received and compared
  • Contractor selected and contract signed
  • Remediation work completed
  • Post-repair verification after next rain event
  • Warranty and invoice filed for records

Key Reminders

  • Do not attempt DIY waterproofing on structural water intrusion — professional assessment is required to identify root cause
  • Check if homeowner's or commercial property insurance covers any portion of the damage or remediation
  • If mold is visible or suspected, request a mold assessment as part of the inspection scope
  • Keep all receipts and documentation for insurance claims or tax purposes
