3 Shipping Process — Step by Step

When a New Order Comes In

  1. Receive the PO — Orders come via email, phone, or directly in QuickBooks. Log every PO in the PO Tracker immediately.
  2. Verify Product Availability — Check inventory and confirm we can fulfill. If items are backordered, notify the customer immediately.
  3. Create Shipping Document — Use the Shipping Documents page to generate the appropriate document (BOL, packing list, etc.).
  4. Schedule Carrier Pickup — Contact our carrier partners (primarily freight for large items, FedEx/UPS for smaller). Confirm pickup date and time.
  5. Update Shipping Log — Log all shipment details: carrier, tracking number, estimated delivery, customer contact.
  6. Notify Customer — Send tracking information to the customer via email with expected delivery date.
  7. Follow Up on Delivery — Monitor tracking and confirm successful delivery. Note any issues immediately.
Pro Tip: Always double-check shipping addresses before generating documents. Address errors are the #1 cause of delivery delays.

4 QuickBooks Invoicing Rules

Invoice Creation Guidelines

  • Invoice Timing: Create invoices after shipment confirmation, not before
  • PO Reference: Always include the customer's PO number in the invoice
  • Terms: Standard terms are Net 30 unless otherwise specified by Aaron
  • Shipping Charges: Add freight costs to the invoice (if customer-paid)
  • Tax: Apply appropriate sales tax based on shipping destination
Important: Never create or send an invoice without verifying shipment has occurred. Invoice discrepancies cause payment delays and customer frustration.

5 What Needs Aaron's Approval

Always Check with Aaron Before:

  • Discounts or Price Adjustments — Any deviation from listed pricing
  • Extended Payment Terms — Anything beyond Net 30
  • Large Orders ($10,000+) — Verify inventory and shipping arrangements
  • New Customer Accounts — Credit terms for first-time customers
  • Returns or Refunds — All return authorizations require approval
  • Vendor Payments — Any payment to suppliers or contractors
  • Equipment Purchases — Office supplies over $100, any equipment
When in doubt, ask. It's always better to confirm than to make an assumption that causes problems downstream.
